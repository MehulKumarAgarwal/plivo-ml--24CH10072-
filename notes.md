My model relies primarily on pitch (F0) slope and energy decay over the final 1.5 seconds of voiced speech. By tracking whether the pitch remains flat/rising rather than falling, and whether energy trails off gradually rather than dropping sharply, the model effectively isolates conversational holds.
The model still fails when generalizing across languages, as seen by the performance drop when evaluating the English-trained model on the Hindi dataset. It also likely struggles with sudden background noises that artificially spike the energy features right before a pause.
With one more day, I would combine the English and Hindi datasets to train a more language-agnostic classifier. I would also experiment with extracting speaking-rate context and final-syllable lengthening to make the feature set more robust against varying speech patterns.
