End-of-Turn (EOT) Detection Assignment Summary

Objective: Build a machine learning model from scratch to predict whether a user has finished their conversational turn, strictly adhering to causality rules (no future audio) and hardware constraints (CPU-only, no pretrained models).

1. The Solution & Why It Beats the Status Quo

The status quo (baseline) utilizes a naive Voice Activity Detection (VAD) endpointing approach, which assumes every pause is an end-of-turn and relies entirely on a rigid 1.6-second silence timer. This results in a frustrating user experience with a ~1600 ms average response delay.

Our solution moves beyond mere silence detection by modeling conversational prosody. By analyzing the 1.5 seconds of audio strictly before the pause, the model captures the acoustic cues humans use to signal intent:

  • Pitch (F0) Dynamics: We calculate the slope of the pitch over the final voiced frames. A falling pitch strongly correlates with a completed thought, whereas a flat or rising pitch signals a conversational "hold."
  • Energy Decay Ratio: Instead of looking at absolute volume, we measure the relative drop in energy. A gradual trailing off indicates hesitation, while a sharp cutoff indicates an end-of-turn.
  • Voicing Fraction: Measures the density of speech leading into the pause to establish pacing context.

These features are fed into a scikit-learn pipeline comprising a StandardScaler and a RandomForestClassifier (or SVC), allowing the system to learn the non-linear boundaries between hesitations and actual completions.

2. Results

By relying on prosodic cues rather than a fixed timer, the pipeline drastically reduced the mean response delay while strictly adhering to the ≤ 5% false-cutoff constraint.

3. Division of Labor (Human vs. AI)

Human Work

  • Audited the baseline failure modes by manually isolating and listening to the longest "hold" pauses in the raw .wav files.
  • Identified the critical acoustic signals (flat pitch, trailing energy) required to separate classes.
  • Architected the shift from absolute features to relative/normalized features to combat cross-lingual overfitting.
  • Executed local ML training loops, evaluated metrics, and managed strict causality rule compliance.

AI Coding Agent (Gemini)

  • Generated pandas utility scripts to quickly parse labels.csv and filter for edge cases.
  • Translated human acoustic hypotheses into vectorized numpy code for the extract_features function.
  • Provided the boilerplate code for the scikit-learn pipeline, handling StandardScaler integration and model saving via joblib.
  • Drafted the final predict.py script to ensure proper inference on unseen data.
